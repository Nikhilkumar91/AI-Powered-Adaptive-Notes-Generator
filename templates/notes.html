Summary
Neural networks are computational models inspired by the human brain. They consist of interconnected nodes (neurons) that process information and learn patterns from data through training. Neural networks are the foundation of modern deep learning and artificial intelligence systems, capable of recognizing patterns, making predictions, and solving complex problems.
Key Highlights
  • Neural networks mimic the structure of biological neurons
  • Composed of input, hidden, and output layers
  • Learn through backpropagation and gradient descent
  • Applications include image recognition, NLP, and prediction
  • Activation functions introduce non-linearity
Detailed Notes
1. What is a Neural Network?
A neural network is a computational model inspired by the biological neural networks that constitute animal brains. It consists of interconnected artificial neurons that process information using a connectionist approach to computation.
  • Input layer receives data
  • Hidden layers perform computations
  • Output layer produces results
  • Weights and biases adjust during training
2. Structure of a Neural Neuron
An artificial neuron (perceptron) has three main components:
  • Dendrites - Receive input signals from other neurons
  • Cell Body (Soma) - Processes the input signals
  • Axon - Transmits output signals to other neurons
Simplified: Think of it like a factory - inputs come in, processing happens, and output is produced.
3. Learning Algorithms
Neural networks learn through iterative training processes:
  • Forward Propagation: Input data passes through the network
  • Loss Calculation: Error between prediction and actual output
  • Backpropagation: Error propagates backward to update weights
  • Gradient Descent: Optimizes weights to minimize loss
Simplified: The network learns by making mistakes and correcting them, similar to how humans learn from feedback.
4. Types of Neural Networks
  • Feedforward Neural Networks (FNN): Simplest type, data flows in one direction
  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN): Excellent for image processing
  • Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN): Handles sequential data like text and time series
  • Transformers: Modern architecture for NLP tasks
